# What is CRM
  CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a system or process used by businesses to manage interactions with customers and potential customers.

It helps companies:

Store customer information
Track sales and communication
Manage leads and opportunities
Improve customer service
Increase sales and customer relationships
Simple Example

A college can use CRM to:

Track students interested in admission
Save student contact details
Follow up on applications
Convert applicants into enrolled students
Basic CRM Flow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer
This means:

1.Someone shows interest (Lead)
2.Their details are confirmed (Contact)
3.There is a chance of business/admission (Opportunity)
4.They finally buy/join (Customer)

# Why Companies use Salesforce
Companies use Salesforce because it helps them manage customers, sales, marketing, and support in one platform.
Main reasons companies use Salesforce:
- Customer Data Management
Stores all customer information in one place.

- Sales Tracking
Helps sales teams track leads, contacts, and opportunities.

- Automation
Automates emails, follow-ups, approvals, and repetitive tasks.

- Better Customer Service
Support teams can quickly solve customer issues.

- Reports and Dashboards
Managers can see sales performance and business reports easily.

- Cloud Access
Employees can access data from anywhere using the internet.

- Team Collaboration
Different departments can work together using shared customer data.

# CRM terms
- Account
 An Account represents a company, organization, or institution with which a business has a relationship.
Example:
A college
A company
A customer organization

In college admission CRM:
Account = College / University

- Contact
A Contact is a person associated with an account.
It stores details like:
Name
Phone number
Email
Address

Example:
Student
Parent
Employee

In college admission CRM:
Contact = Student or Parent

- Opportunity
An Opportunity is a potential business deal or chance of success.
It shows that there is a possibility of:
Sale
Admission
Purchase
Agreement

Example:
Student application under review
Customer likely to buy a product

In college admission CRM:
Opportunity = Student applying for admission

#Understand Business Flow 
Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

Lead: A person or organization that shows interest in a product or service.
Contact: A qualified person whose details are confirmed and stored for communication.
Opportunity: A potential deal or chance of doing business with the contact.
Customer: The person or organization that finally purchases the product or service.

#Real-Life Mapping(College Admission)
CRM Term	Real-Life Example
Account	College / University
Contact	Student or Parent
